📈 Curriculum Corner: Mathematics

We have successfully wrapped up our unit on Equality and Fractions, which focused on combating some common mathematical misunderstandings:

1. The Meaning of Equality (=)

We emphasized that the equals sign is a relational symbol, not an instruction to "do something" or find an answer. Instead, it shows that the quantity, number, or shape on one side of the symbol is equal to the quantity, number, or shape on the other side. Understanding equality as a relationship is a crucial foundational concept.

2. Understanding Fractions and Area

Students worked with shapes to combat the misconception that for fractions to be equal, they must have the same shape. We focused on the idea that when comparing fractions, we only need to think about equal area; the shape itself is not relevant.

In our "Play" activity, students were challenged to partition squares into fourths in many different ways, discovering that many different shapes could fill one-fourth of the same area. The students were very creative in coming up with ways to make quarters.
